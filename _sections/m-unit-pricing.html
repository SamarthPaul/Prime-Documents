PR-PRC-001

Unit Pricing

The Pricing module's cost-buildup engine. It sums seven cost components (raw material, human resource/labour, packaging, transportation, machine depreciation, gas, and rent/electricity overheads) into a Cost per Unit, then applies one of three pricing methods (Cost Plus / Competitor Based / Value Based) across two channels (Offline / Online) to derive Wholesale and Retail prices. It is the heaviest Golden-Thread consumer in the system, and the Retail Price it produces flows back to the Product Profile variants.
Golden-Thread hub. Pulls RM rate & unit (Raw Materials), labour rate & unit (HR), packaging rate & unit (Packaging), machine depreciation rate (Machinery), rent & electricity per month (Infrastructure / Business Establishment profile), and competitor average prices (Market Research). Pushes the derived Retail Price back to Product Profile variants. Existing-tab cost feeds Existing Unit Pricing; the matching SKU's Optimized-tab cost feeds the Summary's saving comparison.

On-Screen UI Structure

A teal title bar reads Unit Pricing Module with crumb PRIME Rural · Pricing Tools. Below it sits a horizontal tab rail — Summary · Existing · Optimized · Resources · Task Log — the active tab highlighted teal.

The Existing and Optimized tabs each hold a list of collapsible product blocks. Each block has a sea-green header carrying an inline SKU selector (auto-fetched from the Product module), a meta label, and a Remove button; clicking the header collapses/expands the body. The block body is a vertical stack of nine Roman-numbered cost-buildup sections: I Raw Materials, II Human Resource, III Packaging, IV Transportation, V Machine Depreciation, VI Gas, VII Others (Rent + Electricity), VIII Unit Cost, and IX Pricing Strategy.

Sections I, II, III & V are child-table grids (No. / item-select / Qty / Unit / Rate / Amount / delete) with an + Add Row button and a highlighted Subtotal strip. Section IV is two transport inputs + subtotal; Section VI is a single Gas row; Section VII is two overhead blocks (Rent, Electricity) each pulling a from-BE monthly figure. Section VIII is the teal gradient Unit Cost callout stacking Total Input Cost, Total Units Produced, Per-Unit Overheads and Cost per Unit. Section IX has a Pricing Method dropdown driving a two-column channel grid (Offline | Online); each channel card shows only the method block selected (A / B / C) and a gradient summary strip (Offline = Wholesale + Retail, Online = Retail only).

Calculated fields (every Amount, every Subtotal, Cost per Unit, Selling/Wholesale, Retail, Rent/Unit, Electricity/Unit) render italic on cream-deep background, are never editable, and carry a formula tooltip (Rule 1). Fetched fields (Unit, Rate, Rent per month, Electricity per month, Competitor Avg when a scope is picked) show read-only with a "Fetched from X" source note (Rule 2). The Summary tab is a fully read-only roll-up.

Key validations (whole form): a block must have a SKU selected before it counts in Summary roll-ups. Child-table rows need a selected item to contribute; at least one row is required per table (deleting the last row is blocked with “At least one row is required”). Qty, Rate, Markup, Margin and units fields are numeric ≥ 0; blanks are treated as 0. Total Units Produced must be > 0 for Cost per Unit to compute; Units produced per month must be > 0 for Rent/Unit and Electricity/Unit. A Pricing Method must be selected to derive any price; Competitor Based needs a Positioning chosen (and a Competitor Avg, fetched or manual) before Selling computes. Fetched fields (Unit, Rate, Rent/month, Electricity/month, and Competitor Avg when a scope is set) are read-only. All currency in Indian Rupee with Indian number formatting.
