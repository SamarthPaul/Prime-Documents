PR-MKT-001

Market Research

Part of the Market module, the Market Research framework lets an entrepreneur capture the market landscape (Global / India / Meghalaya), map selling channels, research competing brands, interview consumers, run a competitor price scan across Local / State / National markets, and conclude with a SWOT. The per-scope competitor average prices computed here are auto-fetched into the Unit Pricing framework when the entrepreneur chooses the Competitor-Based pricing method.
Golden thread to Unit Pricing: Each pricing table (Offline / Online, per market scope) auto-calculates a Competitor Average Price = mean of all non-zero price rows. These six averages (Local / State / National × Offline / Online) are read-only inputs into Unit Pricing → Competitor-Based method. They are not re-keyed there; they flow from this framework. Captured per product.

On-Screen UI Structure

Top bar: forest-teal header "Market Research" with a "PRIME Rural · Marketing Framework" crumb, and an entrepreneur chip below it (avatar + name auto-fetched from profile).
Tab rail (uppercase pills): Summary · Market Research · Competitive Pricing Assessment · Resources · Task Log. Active tab is teal-filled.
Body: Both the Market Research and Competitive Pricing tabs are product-centric repeaters — the entrepreneur clicks "+ Add Product", picks a product from the Product Master in a collapsible card header, and fills nested sections inside that card. There is no single flat form.
Special widgets: (1) collapsible product cards with a product dropdown in the header; (2) multi-select chip pickers for Countries / States / Meghalaya locations; (3) nested entry repeaters (Channel / Brand / Respondent) each as its own collapsible card; (4) currency inputs with a ₹ prefix; (5) competitor price tables with an auto-calculated average row in the table footer; (6) a 2×2 SWOT quadrant grid; (7) a Summary tab with an overview table, aggregate cards and intervention-flag tiles.
Calculated fields (Product Name, Product Snapshot, Competitor Average Price, all Summary aggregates) render italic on cream-deep background, read-only, with a tooltip per Rule 1.

Key validations (whole form): (1) Each product card must have a product selected from the Product Master before save — "Select a product"; avoid duplicate product cards in the same tab. (2) Channel and Brand entries require Outlet Name / Brand Name respectively. (3) All numeric fields (CAGR %, all Prices) must be ≥ 0 — negative prices rejected ("Price cannot be negative"). (4) Competitor Average Price is system-calculated from non-zero positive prices only and is read-only; it is the value auto-fetched into Unit Pricing → Competitor-Based method — it must never be hand-edited. (5) Purchase Frequency is only valid / shown when "Have you used the product? = Yes". (6) Resources table must retain at least one row. (7) Task Log entries require a Task Name. (8) All Indian currency uses ₹ with Indian number formatting (e.g., ₹8,400 Crore, ₹ 1,00,000).
