PR-LG-002

Logistic Service

Entrepreneur-level framework (Logistics module) that assesses the enterprise’s connectivity and delivery capability and maps every shipping route the entrepreneur uses — leg by leg, with mode, time, cost and contact — computing per-trip, monthly and annual logistics spend. Routes that under-perform are flagged for a service upgrade, bridging into the Required tab.
Route-based model. Unlike the Existing/Required-grid frameworks, Logistic Service is organised around routes. Each route holds a leg table (From → To hops) whose costs sum to Total Cost Per Trip; a Frequency multiplier then yields Monthly Cost = Trip Cost × multiplier and Annual Cost = Monthly Cost × 12. Toggling Service Upgrade Needed? on a route bridges it into Required (Rule 3) carrying the route name, type and pain points across.

On-Screen UI Structure

A teal title bar reads Logistic Service; a horizontal tab rail — Summary · Assessment · Existing · Required · Resources · Task Log · Log Book — sits below, active tab teal, with the entrepreneur chip on every panel (Rule 2).

Assessment captures one-time connectivity facts (road quality, nearest highway / rail / courier hub, last-mile access, main issue, seasonal disruption) plus an Available Services checkbox matrix across four families (Courier / Post, Surface Transport, E-commerce Fulfilment, Community / Govt).

Existing renders each route as a collapsible block (Route Name / Purpose, Route Type, Direction in the header). Section I Route Details is a leg table (From, To, Mode, Time, Cost, Contact) footed by Total Cost Per Trip; a teal calc box shows Frequency → Monthly Cost → Annual Cost. Section II Pain Points offers a pill group + Delay Frequency. An Intervention Need block carries the Service-Upgrade toggle.

Required mirrors each flagged route read-only and captures the recommended upgrade + Implementation Status. Summary is read-only (route overview table, aggregate cards, intervention flags). Resources, Task Log and Log Book close the rail. Trip / Monthly / Annual costs render italic on cream-deep with formula tooltips (Rule 1).

Key validations (whole form): Entrepreneur is required and unique (one Logistic Service form per entrepreneur). Each Existing route needs a Route Name and at least one leg row; leg Cost and route costs must be ≥ 0. Trip Cost (Σ legs), Monthly Cost (Trip × frequency multiplier) and Annual Cost (Monthly × 12) are read-only with formula tooltips (Rule 1). Toggling Service Upgrade ON bridges exactly one Required route; removing it clears the flag (two-way sync). Assessment fields are recorded once at entrepreneur level; Available Services drive which transport modes are realistic for the routes.
