EMI uses standard amortisation: EMI = P × r × (1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1), where r = annual rate ÷ 1200 and n = instalments. When two or more loans are checked, a blended rate and blended tenure are shown (informational); EMI is computed per-loan and summed.

How this calculator works

  1. Contribution per unit = Selling price per unit − Variable cost per unit. Variable cost per unit is derived as Total Expense ÷ Quantity.
  2. Monthly obligation = Total Expense + EMI.
  3. Break-even units = ⌈ Monthly obligation ÷ Contribution per unit ⌉.
  4. Break-even revenue = Break-even units × Selling price per unit.
  5. If Contribution per unit is zero or negative, break-even is shown as Not achievable — selling more units would not cover monthly obligations.
